Osun-Osogbo Sacred Grove

Osun-Osogbo Sacred Grove

This revered park, which honors the fertility goddess Osun of the Yoruba people, is the last of its kind. It contains sculptures, shrines, and works of art. The fertility deity or Orisa is known as Osun in Yoruba legend. After a fight with one of Sango's wives, Osun transformed into the Osun River. Since the deity is still thought to reside within the river, the grove is revered as being sacred. An expedition led by an elephant hunter led to the discovery of the grove. The villagers and the goddess agreed that Osun would protect them provided they constructed a shrine for her and guarded the grove. Women came seeking blessings from the deity, and devotees offered offerings to her.

Our own take

By creating massive sculptures made of iron and mud, an Austrian woman by the name of Susan Wenger and several other artists prevented the destruction of the grove. She was hitched to a local drummer and spent the rest of her days in Osun Oshogbo. In 2005, UNESCO designated the Osun-Osogbo Grove as a World Heritage Site.





Conclusion

In a small community on the banks of the Osun River. $3 is the entrance cost. There is an additional $10 camera fee if you bring one in. It is advised to hire a guide (agree with them beforehand, pay about $2) so that you can comprehend the significance and history completely. Be mindful that there are frequently purification ceremonies in the river, so avoid taking pictures during those times.
